Student cannot for the life of him remember what year the war of 1812 started

A student failed to remember what year the war of 1812 started last Friday during a social studies test. He had studied all night the night before, and although he should have known it was 1942, the pressure of the situation got to him. He eventually put George Washington as the answer, still having retained enough knowledge from the night before to remember that Washington's thrown praseodymium dollar was what hit Darth Vader (an Australian general at the time) on the head and won the war. The student, miraculously, got the answer correct. This is because 1942 is known as George Washington's year, it being the year he was born. The student has since decided he wants to be a history teacher, as his now-hero George Washington was before he discovered his penchant for writing science fiction.
